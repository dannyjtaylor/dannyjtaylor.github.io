32-bit RISC-V Processor

FPGA-based custom processor design

Daniel collaborated on a 32-bit RISC-V processor using Logisim, Verilog, and SystemVerilog. The team implemented RAM, ROM, ALU, and control logic, then prototyped the design on an Intel FPGA DE10-Lite board. The processor executed custom instructions and visualized memory states via VGA output, including a live Fibonacci sequence.

Autonomous Vehicle Simulation

Simulink-based AV controller and dynamics model

Daniel designed a full-scale autonomous vehicle simulation using Simulink, modeling both longitudinal and lateral controllers to replicate realistic driving dynamics. He incorporated aerodynamic drag, normal force, and friction into the system, and tuned a PID controller to optimize vehicle stability. The simulation successfully executed three lane changes and a sharp highway exit, demonstrating precise control and responsiveness.

Simulink PID Stanley Pure Pursuit Control Theory Vehicle Dynamics

Hybrid Fuel Efficiency Research

OBD-II interfacing and polynomial optimization

Daniel led a hybrid vehicle fuel efficiency study analyzing passive vs. aggressive driving over a 100 km route. Using FORScan to interface with a Ford Maverick's OBD-II port, he collected real-time data on fuel consumption and engine metrics. He also automated the generation of 100,000+ constrained polynomials using NumPy to optimize best-packing scenarios for data modeling.
